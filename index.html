When science and problem-solving blend with creativity and experiences, creating beautiful forms which meaningfully accompany their functions is incredibly easier. No matter trends, or time, if style adequately matches the purpose of function timeless design comes to light.

I always strive for essentiality within function, and believe truly outstanding experiences are achieved when we manage to meld the essential and functionality within each design solution.

This belief led me to focus. Focus within form & function, focus as a goal. Today more than ever in history our lives are at the mercy of noise.

Quarno
Quarno for iPhone & iPad
Distraction-free Writing

I believe this is terribly wrong. I think we need to create and maintain purposeful relationships between focus and design.

Quarno

Essential elements only, noise-free environment, clear doors & paths, lowest learning barrier possible, and ease of use.

Quarno

I also think openness is key to design. Openness to change, to experiences, openness to the world: I believe that while form may be inspired by form itself, the meaningful links that power the function it follows can be enhanced by opening ourselves up to the world.

Widening our knowledge of the world broadens our design horizons. If what we make is filled with the blend of knowledge in the world and knowledge of design, we are great designers.

Vivo

I hence believe learning creates space for experiences, and if we unlock the doors of the mind, experiences lead to the deepest understanding of the whole environment: platforms, systems, mediums, and most importantly human beings.

I designed for iOS, Web, and Print; coded in and worked with HTML, CSS (and LESS), Objective-C, Python, Rails, Sinatra, Lua, Meteor.js, jQuery, and hence know basic Ruby, JavaScript and C; also write shell scripts and AppleScripts (OS X automation).

I'm 22. At 17 I wrote an iPhone game in Lua (tappixels), which led me, a few months later, to found a startup (dayri). For the following year or so I led a team of four 17, 25, 26, we failed but it was a unique and life-changing experience. Other than managing the team, I worked on the design, helped with the backend (Rails), wrote the frontend, and made a video.

At 19 I designed and developed a series of iOS apps and put together some web app prototypes; in 2013 I worked for iubenda for a few months: I designed a new framework for their landing pages, handled the front-end (written in LESS in an ERB-based Rails environment) and managed a few side-projects.

  • iubenda One of the most promising italian startups. Design, front-end (Rails environment), and two side-projects.
  • Joy A simple iPad app to browse photos from Dropbox. It's on GitHub.
  • Flagello.co (v1) Designed to give the internet world some insights about me by briefly mentioning what I do. Fully responsive, SVG, and CSS3 animations.
  • Flagello.co (v2) A diverse version of the above.
  • Quarno An extremely simple writing app for iPhone and iPad. It's reduced to the very basics. Distraction-free writing in its ultimate sense. Yep, GitHub. The screenshots above show how the new version would have looked like.
  • Vivo An iPhone app made expressly for focus. With a gentle reminder, it helps to get back to the important things in the days focus struggles to show up. GitHub.
  • Veerare Fullscreen and mono-tab browser for iPad. It brings focus to the browsing experience. GitHub.
  • Kalk A minimal calculator for iPad. Built to explore the iPad landscape experience. GitHub.
  • Deebee A super-alpha iOS wrapper for Dropbox SDK. While coding Joy I found myself struggling with Dropbox API and built this little, buggy solution. Discontinued due to their Sync API, but it is obviously on the magnificent lands of GitHub.

  • tappixels First iOS thing. A game I designed and developed in Lua at 17. It's no more on the Store, but the video I made to promote it is still up.
